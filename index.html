Hi there!

I’m Anahita Bolourani (Ana), a Ph.D. candidate at UCLA who is passionate about artificial intelligence—especially diffusion models and large language models. My research blends advanced statistical theory with state‑of‑the‑art machine‑learning tools such as PyTorch and TensorFlow to push the limits of how we model, generate, and understand complex data. From image synthesis to seismic analysis and emotion‑aware systems, I explore ways generative models can drive real‑world impact.

Welcome to my GitHub page, where you’ll find my latest projects, publications, and occasional reflections on the future of AI.

Teaching

I have more than three years of classroom experience, spanning two universities and a range of subjects from introduction to statistics and probability to graduate‑level ground motion characterization. At UCLA I have served as a teaching assistant for CEE 125, CEE 125, and CEE 245, guiding teams on numerical methods and programming workflows in MATLAB and Python. My teaching philosophy centres on active learning, inclusive discussion, and real‑world engineering case studies—skills I bring to every Zoom session and office hour.
